The Haj and Umra Higher Committee (HUHC) has suspended the licence of one  Haj and Umra operator and sanctioned another 14 for failing to adhere to the conditions they signed last year with the GAIAE concerning providing services of good quality to pilgrims.
This came in a recent meeting presided over by HE Dr Hamdan Musallam Al Mazrouei, Chairman of the  GAIAE's and the HUHC, and was attended by representatives of different ministries and federal authorities as well as the  director of Haj and Umra Department at the GAIAE.
After careful examination of violation cases brought to it, the committee decided to withdraw the licence of one operator and ordered it to stop providing Haj and Umra services. Another 14 operators have also been sanctioned for mismanagement, lack of hygiene and violation of housing conditions.  Size of fines and level of warnings issued varied according to the type and seriousness of the contravention of service agreements with the GAIAE.
The committee  hailed the great support the GAIAE and the official Haj delegation had always received from HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the UAE President, may Allah protect him, HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, the Vice President, Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai, Their Highness Members of the Supreme Council, the Rulers of the Emirates, General Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces.
The committee highlighted that the special interest of the Prudent UAE leadership in the Committee and the official delegation had greatly benefitted the quality of services offered to pilgrims during their stay in the Holy Shrines.
The HUHC consists of the ministries of Health and the Interior,  the General Authority of Youth and Sports Welfare represented by the Emirates Scout Association, Dubai's Islamic Affairs and Charitable Activities Department in addition to the GAIAE.
